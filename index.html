What is a Compount Interest?

Compound interest is when your money earns interest not just on the original amount you put in (the principal) but also on the interest it’s already earned. Think of it like a snowball rolling down a hill—it starts small, but as it keeps rolling, it picks up more and more snow, growing faster and faster. For example, if you invest ₱10,000 at 5% annual interest, you’ll earn ₱500 in the first year, giving you ₱10,500. In the second year, that 5% is calculated on ₱10,500, so you earn ₱525, and it keeps building from there. Over time, this “interest on interest” effect can turn small investments into something much bigger, making compound interest one of the most powerful tools for growing your money.
